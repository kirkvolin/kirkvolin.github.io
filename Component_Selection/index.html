    For a power regulator, the LM2676S-3.3 seems to be the optimal solution. It offers a good combination of price, usability, and versatility. Because of it's lower minimum input voltage of 4.5V, it enables the use of USB as a source of power compared to the LM2675M and LM2676S which wouldn't be able to accomplish this due to their minimum requirements of 6.5V and 8V respectively. The regulator's size of the TO263CA standard will also be much easier to manipulate and place for surface mount hand soldering.

    For powering this subsystem, I feel that versatility is important. Because USB is already required to program the ESP32 chip, I will design the circuit board so that it can be powered from either the USB connector or a separate 5.5x2.5mm barrel jack which is a common standard that my teammates and I already have cables for through the Robotics 1 and 2 courses through the Robotics 1 and 2 courses.
